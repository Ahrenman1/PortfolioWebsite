MioTag: Stroke Rehabilitation

This project served as my Master's thesis at the University of Strathclyde. It was completed alongside my teammates, Filip Lejhanec, Wojtek Cichoradzki, Fraser Martin and Leo McKeever.

The aim of the project was to develop a smart glove which connects with a mobile device to creater an interactive platform to assist with the rehabilitation of nerve damage victims, primarily stroke patients.

I developed the concept for the platform in late 2018, and spent my dissertation (Wi-Fi Activity Monitor) building the foundations, before forming a strong team and pitching the idea to the University department for my Master's project. I was the project manager and lead hardware developer of the project, and found the development process to be very fulfilling and creative.

After noticing the difficulty surrounding stroke rehabilitation, such as long wait times, few available appointments, and a lack of compelling rehabilitation exercises, I devised the platform to provide a much cheaper and more engaging experience. The user can complete exercises and participate in games while wearing the glove, and then log these activities to their account to be viewed by themselves (to improve motivation) and their clinicians (to track progress). The app is also social, with friends and family able to connect with each other to provide a community of support.

As the project lead, I was responsible for guiding the outcome in the right direction, using my initial vision of what the platform could become as inspiration. I was solely responsible for the design and manufacture of the glove frame, which housed the electronics. This involved using CAD extensively to develop not only the frame, but inverse of the frame to make the mould for the rubber to be poured in. I was also largely involved with the electronic development, as I selected all the components and built the PCB on EasyEDA in conjunction with Leo McKeever and Filip Lejhanec.

The final product was of a very high standard and conformed to the requirements set out initially, even in the midst of a global pandemic. The elastic rubber that was used in the frame allowed for the glove to be universally sized, meaning it was as inclusive as possible.
