More, Mini Projects

This section will detail other projects that I have been completed, albeit in less detail than the other major projects listed in the previous webpage. If you would like to know more about them, please feel free to start a conversation using the contact page in this website.

CAD Marble Run

This aim of this project was to mechanically design and test a marble run in SolidWorks. It involved seven unique phases, including a spiral, upwards staircase, loop the loop etc. The motion study performed had to capture the entire lifecycle of the marble, and had to return to the start position. The project was also open ended, meaning I could be creative my design decisions.

Beijing Pollution Deep Learning

This was a group systems engineering task to develop several neural networks to try to predict the level of pollution in a given area in Beijing based on easy to measure environmental factors (rainfall, temperature etc). This was to mitigate the use of expensive sensors to calculate exact pollution levels. My main task was to model the multi-layer perceptron classification network, and the deep learning regression network, to produce results that were similar to the actual pollution levels.

Obstacle Avoiding Robot

The objective of this project was to modify a toy car to navigate an obstacle course, avoiding white lines and walls, to reach a finish line. it was done with a team. This was a competition against other classmates to see who could complete the course, and who could do it the fastest. My main task was building and fitting a frame around the robot that housed the sensors and electronics, so that none of the design rules were broken. I was also heavily involved with coding the STM32 Blue Pill microcontroller within the car. Our group came 5th out of 12 teams.

Deep Learning Posture Assessment

This was a team project that was tasked with designing and testing a deep learning network capable of categorising images into distinct categories of posture, to aid in rehabilitation and nursing homes. The optimised cost function of the stochastic gradient descent method (SGD) was used to train image datasets of 4 different categories. The network was purposefully made small to reduce the latency of inputting an image and receiving an output, in order to produce live feedback of a video stream from a webcam.
