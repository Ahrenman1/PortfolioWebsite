Sound Following Robot

This is a team project I completed in my third year of University. The goal was to develop a robot that was capable of following noise of a specific frequency. The robot was to be intelligent enough to disregard frequencies that it was not tuned to find, and also have obstacle avoidance capabilities. The project was very open creatively, and allowed us to put our own spin on the design and functionality of the robot.

A Raspberry Pi 3 was selected to process the sound and control the motors. In the Raspberry Pi, the Idle IDE was used, hence Python was the programming language running in the robot. A mini USB microphone, Ultrasound sensor and L293D motor controller were integrated to make the robot fully operational.

My role in the team was project manager and programming lead. I was responsible for the building, integration and testing of components to produce the final result. It was exciting to have practical applications of skills developed throughout University, and my ability in Python improved substantially, as well as my interpersonal skills in a team dynamic.

The result of the project was a fully autonomous robot capable of being tuned to follow any frequency between 100Hz and 20,000Hz, and with obstacle avoidance algorithms built in. A creative twist on similar projects was the small size of the robot, allowing it more mobility and less power consumption. The demonstration and presentation went excellently, with the robot conforming perfectly to the initial brief and requirements. The team received a high mark for this work.
